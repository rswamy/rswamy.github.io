01 SupplyTrust · 2025
Customer Discovery Problem Definition Adoption

AI Workflow Tools for Supply Chain Analysts

Turning a day of manual research into a 3-minute workflow.

The Problem When I joined SupplyTrust as a contract analyst, I needed to get up to speed on a large corpus of supplier information quickly in order to run assessments. The process was painful: analysts spent up to a full day manually searching through notes, past visit reports, and documents to surface relevant insights. There was no good way to connect information across years of supplier history.
My Role I built AI workflows to solve my own problem first, then presented them to leadership. They saw the broader opportunity and I took ownership: defining requirements, building the backend, and shipping a Google Docs extension so any analyst could use the tools without technical expertise.
How I Built It I used Vertex AI and Gemini API to build document processing tools and a RAG-based knowledge system that resurfaces insights across SupplyTrust's supplier database. I ran evaluation frameworks and iterative feedback sessions with analysts to validate and improve reliability.
Driving Adoption By packaging everything as a Google Docs extension, I met analysts in the tool they already used. The real adoption signal: when the tool broke, analysts messaged me to fix it rather than reverting to manual work.

02 3D Fab Light · 2016–2024
Roadmap Management Requirements & Specs Stakeholder Alignment

Advanced Manufacturing Software - No Training Required

Made a $110K machine as easy to use as an office printer.

The Problem Competing laser cutters required a week of on-site training on top of machine cost - and created a dangerous single point of failure. If the one trained operator called in sick, a $110K machine sat idle. I set out to make our CAM and HMI software so intuitive that any operator could use it from day one.
My Role I owned the full product lifecycle for both our CAM software and the machine HMI—from user research through spec writing, contractor management, sprint execution, and user testing. I was the connective tissue between what customers needed and what got built.
How I Built It I visited 20+ manufacturing facilities to observe operators in real conditions. I tracked feature requests via Freshdesk and Trello, wrote detailed specs for software contractors, and managed weekly sprints with internal user testing before every release.
Roadmap Process Every feature request cleared three filters: Does this affect our ability to close or retain a customer? Does it reduce field failure rate? Does it unblock the team? I kept the backlog visible so stakeholders always understood the prioritization reasoning.

03 3D Fab Light · 2018–2020
0-to-1 Product Thinking Technical Scoping Alpha Launch

IoT Platform for Connected Machine Management

From manual service calls to real-time machine visibility.

The Problem Every remote service call required customers to download TeamViewer, find an Ethernet cable, and connect a laptop to the machine. The process was slow, frustrating, and unreliable, and there was no way to proactively monitor machine health in the field.
My Role I identified the problem with my co-founder and owned product definition: what the platform needed to do, what secure remote access should look like, and what zero-setup meant for the customer. I scoped the work, engaged a contractor, and managed through to alpha launch.
How I Built It Working with a contractor, I defined requirements for a custom Node.js IoT platform that put machines online securely - enabling remote diagnostics with no customer setup burden and wireless job transmission from any networked computer.
Launch & Learning We launched to a select alpha group to validate core capabilities. Remote servicing became dramatically faster, and post-launch telemetry gave us real-time visibility into machine health - error codes, temperatures, operating hours - for the first time.
